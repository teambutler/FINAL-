MY jouRNeY

   I started out as a Mechanical Engineering major at Southern University in Baton Rougge, LA with aspirations to work in the engineering field. Nevertheless I worked for 3 1/2 years and decided that this was not my passion and changed schools and my major. Subsequently, I migrated to Georgia State University in Atlanta, Gallery where I was intrigued with the Criminal Justice field and my passion to read and analyze the law. I wanted to pursue a career in the legal field as an attorney but life events happen and I was not able to do that but I never forgot about the passion I had for reading and speaking. Moving forward in my life I was married and entered into the military where I served as a Captain in the US Army where I did 2 tours to Iraq in support of combat operations (OIF). This was one of my proudest moments and I realized that I had the passion to serve others and to motivate others to be the best they could be while at the same time pushing myself past my limits. After leaving the military with a Honorable Discharge I decided that my life's work had to be in anything that I could do to help people. I am a people person so that would allow me to work in any field that I desired while at the same time stretching myself mentally. I worked for the Department of Human Services in Atlanta, GA where I was a program manager responsible for ensuring that foster youth received high quality tutoring services. Next, I decided to take a position with the Atlanta Public Schools in the area of federal grants as a Compliance Coordinator. In this position I was responsible for ensuring that all Title one schools expended their federal funds according to federal guidelines and in a timely manner. This was an outstanding position because it allowed me to work closely with the administrations at various schools and I had an opportunity to speak to students. The next position I moved into after that at Atlanta Public Schools was a federal grants specialist where I assisted schools with understanding how to expend their federal funds during the fiscal year. I have worked at various jobs after the above positions, never really finding that position that would feed my passion for working with others or that was challenging to me at this position in my life. Hence, my journey to TrueCoders and the challenge to become a software developer. This has definitely been a challenge to me because it has made me think outside of my comfort zone while at the same time making me work with others who have similar backgrounds--wanting to be software developers. I am sure that this journey will be forever changing and always challenging to me but I will never give up my passion to wanting to be the best at what I do and to serve others.
